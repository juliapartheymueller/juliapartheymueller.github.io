Department of Government

Vienna Center for Electoral Research (VieCER)

Julia Partheymüller is a Senior Scientist at the Department of Government, University of Vienna, and a member of the Vienna Center for Electoral Research (VieCER). Her research focuses on electoral behaviour, political communication, public opinion, and democratic attitudes in Austria and comparative European contexts.

She previously worked as a DAAD Lecturer at the University of Essex and was part of the German Longitudinal Election Study (GLES) team at the Mannheim Centre for European Social Research (MZES), University of Mannheim. She holds a doctoral degree in the social sciences from the University of Mannheim and studied political science at Freie Universität Berlin and the University of Hamburg.

Research interests: Political communication and voting behaviour; campaign effects; dynamics of media coverage and public opinion; traditional and new media; social communication and interpersonal influence; social networks and political participation; comparative politics; European politics; democracy in the European Union; quantitative methods; survey research; content analysis.
